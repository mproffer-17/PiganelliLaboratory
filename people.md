# Production Crew

The Piganelli Lab is a collaborative research group at Indiana University School of Medicine studying type 1 diabetes, redox immunology, T cell metabolism, beta-cell stress, and early immune biomarkers.

    <h3>Jon D. Piganelli, PhD</h3>

<p><a class="button" href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/jon-piganelli-076505212/" target="_blank">LinkedIn Profile</a></p>

<p>
Dr. Jon D. Piganelli is a Professor of Medicine in the Department of Endocrinology at Indiana University School of Medicine, where he leads a research program focused on the immune-mediated pathogenesis of type 1 diabetes. His work centers on T cell–mediated beta-cell destruction, redox signaling, oxidative stress, immunometabolism, beta-cell stress, and therapeutic strategies designed to slow, prevent, or reverse disease progression. His scientific background spans immunology, microbiology, biochemistry, metabolism, endocrinology, oxidative stress, and free radical biology, making him a widely sought source of advice across collaborative research spaces. IU notes that he joined IU School of Medicine in 2023 after overseeing a well-funded research program at the University of Pittsburgh for 22 years, and that his work has been supported by the NIH, American Diabetes Association, and JDRF/Breakthrough T1D.

With more than 35 years of scientific training, Dr. Piganelli has contributed significantly meaningful research to the fields of type 1 diabetes, autoimmunity, redox biology, immunometabolism, and beta-cell stress. His lab reflects both the depth of his scientific expertise and his commitment to building an inclusive, collaborative environment where trainees, staff, and scientists from different backgrounds can grow together. He has mentored postdoctoral fellows, graduate students, medical fellows, undergraduate students, and national and international trainees throughout his career.

Dr. Piganelli can almost always be found in his office listening to golden oldies, classic rock, and nostalgic throwbacks while zoning in on the grant writing that keeps the lab’s research moving forward. On his off time, Dr. Piganelli loves to research new discoveries in astrophysics research and excitedly sharing his findings with us. He is also known for assigning nicknames to almost everyone and somehow never forgetting them. His generosity with his time, his instinct to put others first, and his commitment to bringing people into science are central to the culture of the Piganelli Lab.
</p>
